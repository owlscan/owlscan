# Code of Conduct — NightOwl // PHANTOM SIGNAL

> *This project exists to advance legitimate security research. That purpose is non-negotiable.*

---

## Preamble

NightOwl is an intelligence framework with real capability. The people who build and use it operate in a domain where the line between research and harm is drawn by intent, authorization, and discipline — not by the tool itself.

This Code of Conduct is not a formality. It is the terms under which this community operates. Violations are taken seriously, reviewed without sentiment, and acted upon.

---

## Scope

This Code applies to all spaces associated with NightOwl: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, the project wiki, and any other forum where you represent this project or its community. It applies to maintainers, contributors, and users who engage publicly in this space.

---

## Standards

### Expected

- **Operate within authorization.** Never discuss, share, or celebrate the use of NightOwl — or any derivative — against systems, organizations, or individuals without documented, explicit permission.
- **Communicate with precision.** This community values clear, technical, professional communication. Be direct. Be accurate. Be concise.
- **Disclose responsibly.** If you discover a vulnerability in NightOwl itself, follow the process in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md). Do not post it publicly before a fix is available.
- **Respect the research.** Critique ideas, not people. Disagree with evidence, not volume. Assume competence and good intent until demonstrated otherwise.
- **Protect your sources.** Do not share real target data, scan output against live systems, or personally identifiable information in Issues, PRs, or Discussions — even as an example.

### Not Tolerated

- Use of this project for unauthorized reconnaissance, surveillance, harassment, stalking, or any activity that would constitute a criminal offence under applicable law.
- Sharing, discussing, or implying results obtained from unauthorized scans of real infrastructure.
- Discrimination, harassment, or hostile behavior on the basis of identity, background, or skill level.
- Doxxing, intimidation, or coordinated attacks on individuals — inside or outside this community.
- Deliberate introduction of malicious code, backdoors, or data-exfiltration mechanisms into this codebase or its dependencies.
- Impersonation of maintainers, contributors, or any third party.

---

## Enforcement

Reports are reviewed by the project maintainers. If a conflict of interest exists, an independent reviewer will be engaged.

**The enforcement ladder is:**

| Severity | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Minor / first offence | Private warning with explanation |
| Repeated or moderate | Temporary ban from project spaces |
| Severe or deliberate | Permanent ban; referral where appropriate |

There is no appeal process for bans resulting from illegal use of the software.

**Maintainers will not:**
- Debate enforcement decisions publicly
- Negotiate terms for reinstatement following a severe ban
- Provide advance notice before removing content that violates this Code

## Final Note

This community is small, skilled, and serious. The capabilities this project provides carry weight. That weight demands proportional discipline — from every person who contributes a line of code, files an issue, or uses the tool in the field.

Act accordingly.
